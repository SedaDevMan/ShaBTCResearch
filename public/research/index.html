Toy N=1 vs Real Haraka-512 — Avalanche & Correlation

Why MIX512 is decisive: The 4×128-bit parallel AES block structure is interleaved by MIX512 after each round, meaning every input bit propagates across all 512 output bits within the first round. This achieves full diffusion even with a single AES round — the toy N=1 flaw (no inter-block mixing) is architecturally impossible in real Haraka-512.

Overall Conclusion
What we confirmed:

• All production mining algorithms (SHA256d, Haraka-512, HeavyHash, RandomX) are statistically indistinguishable from ideal random oracles under all tested attacks.

• No cross-block-of-time nonce carry-over observed in any algorithm.

• Methodology is validated: detects provably leaky hashes (xor_shift p=0.000, VerusHash N=1 r=0.354) and correctly finds nothing on secure algorithms.
What remains open:

• The algebraic speedup is real for xxHash64 (non-crypto). If a real mining algorithm were to expose an invertible sub-component, speedup scales exponentially with difficulty.

• VerusHash 2.1 rounds <4 are exploitable — real hardware must implement ≥4 rounds to be secure.

• Mining profitability timing (ETC difficulty spikes) represents the most actionable near-term edge: no hash math required.
